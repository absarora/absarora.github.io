Dealing with Conflict

  1. Discuss a time you had an emotional conflict with someone. What was it about? What happened?

    I had an emotional conflict with my best friend, I had to move away for higher studies and she couldn't accept the fact that we would not be able to see each other for a long time.

  2. What was the source of the conflict?

    We met in a cooking class, we hit it off immediately we had the same interests, the same hobbies, and we were close friends for 6 years. We saw each other almost everyday; she thought we would lose touch, she wasn't supportive of my decision or my dream of pursuing higher studies.

  3. How did you handle that conflict?

    I tried explaining her as why it was so important, I would try and convince her as to why I needed to leave. I then showed her my acceptance letter.

  4. Did your actions make the conflict better or worse?

    She refused to understand, we fought everyday. But then one day she saw my acceptance letter from the university and she understood the importance of the situation.

  5. If you could go back, what would you do differently, if anything?

    I would have been more patient with her, more understanding.

  6. What did you learn from this experience?

    I learnt how there are so many people who depend on you and your decisions, how your actions have consequences that are sometimes faced by the ones who care for you.
